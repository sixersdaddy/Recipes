# Fajitas (Steak)

Based on memories of Ed Van Meter’s steak fajita marinade. Quantities are **estimates**.

**Course:** dinner, fajitas
**Cuisine:** Tex-Mex

**Prep Time:** 30-60 min
**Marinade Time:** 1-12 hrs
**Cook Time:** 30 min
**Total Time:** 2-14 hrs

**Servings:** 4
**Calories:** TBD kcal
**Author:** Doc / doc@wvfans.net


## Ingredients

- 1 lb skirt steak (prefer blade tenderized)
- 1 bottle Worcestershire sauce (typ. 15-18 oz)
- 1 bottle A.1. sauce (typ. 10-15 oz) - I prefer Tabasco Bold & Spicy variety, though Original is tasty as well
- 1/4 cup (est.) Vegetable oil
- Vinegar - to taste preference (will add tartness)
- Lime Juice - to taste preference (will add tartness) - regular or key lime is OK
- Garlic - to taste preference - OK to use freshly minced garlic or dried minced garlic
- Onion - to taste preference - OK to use finely diced onions (provides flavor & texture) or chopped onion flakes (provides more of a texture and less of a flavor)
- Salt - to taste preference - Kosher or sea salt
- Feel free to add a splash of any other spice or flavoring to accent the taste


## Instructions

1. FREEZE skirt steak for 30-60 minutes to stiffen meat for slicing
2. SLICE skirt steak into thin strips (2-4 mm thick), cutting across the grain direction
3. ADD skirt steak and all ingredients into marinade container; meat should be 90-100% submerged in marinade (ADD marinade ingredients, if necessary)
4. CLOSE container and SHAKE vigorously to thoroughly mix ingredients; MARINATE in refrigerator for 1-12 hrs
6. Periodically SHAKE / FLIP marinade container to ensure meat is fully covered
7. COOK skirt steak on hot griddle or grill until it’s medium to well-done
8. SERVE cooked skirt steak on warm tortilla shells
9. TOP with desired condiments - salsa, guacamole, sour cream, shredded cheese, etc.


## Notes

**Storing:** Refrigerate up to a week in a container fitted with a lid.
